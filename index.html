Privacy Policy

Extension: Transcribe Video to Text
Last updated: July 2, 2026

1. Overview

Transcribe Video to Text is a Chrome browser extension that transcribes and summarizes audio and video content. We are committed to protecting your privacy. This policy describes all data collected, how it is used, stored, and shared.

2. Data We Collect

  • Audio data: When native subtitles are unavailable, audio from the active tab is temporarily captured and sent to a third-party transcription service (Deepgram or Speechmatics) for processing. Audio is not stored on our servers after transcription.
  • Usage token: A randomly generated UUID is created locally and stored in your browser. It is sent to our backend server with each request to track your usage credits. It is not linked to any personal identity.
  • Browser fingerprint: A hash derived from browser properties (e.g. language, screen resolution, timezone) is generated locally and sent to our backend solely to detect abuse and enforce fair usage limits. It is not used for advertising or profiling.
  • Video metadata and subtitle text (YouTube): When a YouTube video has caption tracks, the extension fetches subtitle text directly from YouTube servers and displays it locally. For usage accounting purposes, the video URL, detected language, video duration, and subtitle text are sent to our backend server. This data is used solely to record credit consumption and is not shared with third parties.
  • Video metadata (other platforms): For non-YouTube videos, video URL and platform type are sent to our backend as part of the transcription request. Subtitle text from Vimeo and other platforms is fetched locally and not transmitted to our servers.
  • Uninstall feedback (optional): When you uninstall the extension, Chrome opens a voluntary feedback survey hosted on Google Forms. Participation is entirely optional. Any data you choose to submit in that form is governed by Google's Privacy Policy. We do not receive any data unless you actively submit the form.

3. Data We Do NOT Collect

  • We do not collect your name, email address, or any personal identifiers.
  • We do not store audio recordings on our servers.
  • We do not track your browsing history outside of active transcription sessions.
  • We do not sell or share your data with third parties for advertising purposes.

4. Data Storage and Retention

  • Local storage: Your usage token, credit balance, and session state are stored in your browser using Chrome's local storage API. This data stays on your device.
  • Backend server: Usage records (video URL, language, duration, subtitle text for YouTube) are stored on our server to enforce usage limits. This data is not linked to any personally identifiable information.
  • Audio: Audio data is transmitted directly to the transcription provider and is not retained after processing.

5. Third-Party Services

When native subtitles are unavailable, audio transcription is processed by one of the following services:

  • Deepgram — primary transcription provider. Audio is transmitted solely for transcription and is subject to their privacy policy.
  • Speechmatics — fallback transcription provider, used automatically if Deepgram is temporarily unavailable. Subject to their privacy policy.

Caption tracks for YouTube and Vimeo videos are fetched directly from those platforms's servers. YouTube subtitle text is additionally sent to our backend for usage accounting as described above.

6. Permissions

  • tabCapture / offscreen: To capture tab audio for transcription when native subtitles are not available.
  • activeTab: To read the current page URL, video metadata, and caption track information.
  • scripting: To detect media presence and extract subtitle tracks from embedded video players (e.g. Vimeo iframes).
  • storage: To store your usage token, credit balance, and transcription state locally in the browser.
  • sidePanel: To display the transcription interface.
  • webNavigation: To detect page navigation and reset transcription state accordingly.

7. Data Security

All data is transmitted over encrypted HTTPS/WSS connections. Audio is not retained after transcription is complete. Usage tokens and fingerprint hashes do not contain personally identifiable information.
